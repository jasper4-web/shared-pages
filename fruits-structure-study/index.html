Answering the one question

Strongest overall balance: S1 now, S3 as the destination, S4 as the counterweight. The honest reading of the matrix: the tuck carton wins the next 90 days (only true pilot path, lowest cost, zero tooling) but never wins the shelf on shape alone. The matchbox drawer is the structure this brand was always pointing at — certified sustainability, a signature ritual, a silhouette break — and it becomes buyable exactly at the reorder-gate volume (5k). The tin is the disciplined alternative if the drawer quotes come back above ~$1.20: cheaper at scale, zero plastic, and a keepsake — its only sin is Kiva adjacency.

The sequencing that risks nothing: ① pilot in S1 (500–1,000 units, this quarter — the structure is invisible in a pilot anyway; demand signal is what's being bought); ② in parallel, get the three S3 quotes (Dymapak SlideSafe · Zenpack Zenlock · SunGrown — all quote-only, all free to ask) plus Dymapak's tin quote, and order ~$60 of physical samples (SlideSafe sample, stock tin, Paper Tube Co canister, CoolJarz tub); ③ at the reorder gate, cut the flagship structure over to the winner — S3 if ≤ ~$1.10 landed, S4 if not — and keep S1 tooling as the value/second line. S6 stays on the bench as the future travel format; S2's tub and S7's case are line-extension formats, not flagships.

What would change this verdict: an S3 quote under $0.90 (cut over immediately — the story is worth more than the delta), or a co-packer who already runs blister sealing (S6 jumps two places). One more honest note: these are vendor photographs, not objects in your hand — before the final call, the winning 2–3 structures should exist on your desk as physical samples (~$60 total). A photo can't tell you how the slide feels or whether the tin's squeeze is satisfying; the hand decides what the eye can't.
