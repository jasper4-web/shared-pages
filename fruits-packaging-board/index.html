06 · Premium look without premium cost

Six plays that keep the FRUITS feel

1
Move the deboss to the LABEL

Blind emboss of the exact locked logo on cream textured estate stock: +$0.10–0.30/label + one $200–1,000 plate — vs. $3+ of container-and-band. The 0.4 mm die-safety work transfers directly.

2
Wyld's move, FRUITS' skin

Soft-touch cream carton, blind deboss or foil on board, certified CR inner. Print "F*CK CORPORATE CANNABIS" inside the carton — a discovery moment, off the compliance surface.

3
Printed sleeve over a stock container

Paperboard belly bands from ~$0.40 @1k, dropping fast at 3k+. One plain container + many sleeve SKUs = flavor variety, zero obsolescence.

4
Digital foil / spot UV under 5k units

No die, setup ~72% cheaper than hot-stamp below 1k. Foil-decorated packs rate >80% stronger on perceived quality.

5
Soft-touch lamination

"The most luxurious finish in cannabis packaging" at a near-zero delta — under $0.02–0.15/unit. Touch drives purchase intent more than any visual finish.

6
Keep Sana as the LIMITED flagship

One case = 500 units = $1,000. Numbered "First 500" at $25+, budtender gifts, influencer seeding — where its story is loudest and its cost is marketing, not COGS.

07 · The owner decision

Pick one strategy — don't average them

Strategy 1

Luxury niche — keep the pack, raise the price

$25–28 shelf · Sana + band, exactly as designed
  • Margin becomes ~$2.75/pack at pilot — healthy, honest
  • 500-unit runs, direct-to-indie-dispensary, Rose-Delights lane
  • Low volume by design; the packaging justifies the price
  • Trade-off: abandons the "competitive price" business goal
Strategy 2 · Recommended

Competitive price — new structure, same soul

$16–18 shelf · carton + CR inner (primary) · embossed-label jar (fallback) · Sana becomes the limited flagship
  • $0.60–1.35/unit packaging → 62–70% gross margins at scale
  • Format proven at #1 in this exact tier (Wyld)
  • Cream / deboss / quiet-luxury language survives on board & label
  • No CR testing — certified inner carries it
  • Vector master, copy sheet, compliance work transfer unchanged
Recommendation: Strategy 2 — Option E (soft-touch cream carton + certified CR inner) as the volume SKU, Option G as fallback, Sana + band preserved as the numbered limited flagship. Nothing already built is wasted.
08 · Cheap verification before committing

~$100 and two emails settle it

  1. Sample kit (<$100): one Wyld carton, one Kiva tin, one BrandMyDispo custom pouch, one Calyx jar — a physical line-up next to the Sana render.
  2. Two real quotes on the chosen format: carton @1k/5k + certified CR inner (Dymapak / BrandMyDispo), and a label converter with blind emboss for Option G.
  3. Ask the co-packer which format their line fills cheapest — pouches fill inline; jars & cartons need more hands.
  4. D23 still proceeds either way: order the Sana case ($1,000) — the flagship needs it and the die order still waits on the physical measurement.
