01 · Why this architecture is legal (verbatim, verified against current DCC regs)

The carton carries the brand. The inner carries the law.

The green light: DCC's own compliance guidance says you may "use any layer of packaging" to meet the packaging requirements (child-resistant · tamper-evident · resealable · opaque), and §17402(c) explicitly contemplates "a container placed inside of a box." The regs put NO child-resistance duty on the box — which is exactly how Wyld and Kiva ship statewide. Your premium carton is legally free to be pure design.
CR lives on the inner — for lifeThe inner must be certified child-resistant (16 CFR 1700.15(b)(1)) and stay CR through the product's life — multi-serving edibles get no "CR until opened" pass. A tear-notch one-time pouch fails; a locking zipper or squeeze-lock tub passes.§17412(a)(1) · §17412(b)(1) — SOLID
Resealable, because 10 servings"If the cannabis good has more than one serving, the package shall be resealable."§17411(a)(3) — SOLID
Opaque — the carton does this job"If the cannabis good is an edible product, the package shall be opaque." An opaque carton satisfies the retail unit; keep the inner opaque too (it's the layer that legally "contains" the product).§17411(a)(5) + DCC any-layer guidance — SOLID
Tamper-evident — either layerSealed so contents can't be accessed "without obvious destruction of the seal." A seal sticker over the carton flap or a film seal on the inner both qualify; the inner seal is the robust choice.§17411(a)(2) — SOLID
Duty: symbol on BOTH layersBecause the inner is separable from the box, the inner container must ALSO carry the universal symbol (≥0.5", printed black or white — a molded or debossed symbol doesn't count).§17402(c) + §17410 — SOLID
Duty: the front panel formula"CANNABIS-INFUSED" in bold, immediately above the product identity, in LARGER type than the identity + the 12.7mm symbol + net weight (metric & US). Cannabinoid mg may live on the informational panel instead of the front.§17404 · §17405 · §17407(a) — SOLID
Duty: what can go on the insertEverything in §17406(a) EXCEPT manufacturer name/contact, UID, and batch/lot may move to a package insert, fold-out, or booklet. QR codes never satisfy required text.§17406(c) — SOLID
Duty: no gummy pictures, no kid-appealThe label may not show a picture of the edible product, use content attractive to under-21s, or name a CA city unless 100% produced there.§17408(a) — SOLID
02 · Anatomy of the pack

Three pieces, each with one job

1 · The carton — the brand's whole canvas. No CR duty, so the structure, board, and finishes are pure design freedom. Legal jobs: opacity, the front-panel formula, informational panel, no gummy imagery.
2 · The certified CR inner — the legal workhorse. A 16 CFR 1700-certified, lifetime-resealable pouch or tub. Legal jobs: child-resistance, resealability, its own printed universal symbol, UID/batch (applied at packout), tamper seal (best here).
3 · The insert — the overflow valve. Government warning, nutrition, instructions, the brand story — everything §17406(c) allows off the panels. Frees the carton to stay quiet.
The strategic consequence: because the law never touches the carton's structure, the die can be pure theater — exactly the trick behind Wyld's "origami" box. Your deboss, your interior message, your unboxing moment: all unregulated.

Feasible — expect slightly softened hairlines, and have the die engraver review the art before cutting. Deboss is more forgiving than emboss on cartons, which is why the spec says deboss.
