Can my AI actually run my grow room?

Yes — the watching half is solved and reliable today. The "AI runs everything by itself, unsupervised" half is real but still hobbyist-grade and needs babysitting. Here's exactly where the line sits — built around your exact room, not generic gear.

Your room · the straight answer
Do you need an all‑AC‑Infinity setup? No — and for your gear, AC Infinity is the wrong buy.
AC Infinity can switch your own plug‑in devices on/off — through one accessory, the UIS Control Plug. But it's a dumb slave that needs a ~$90 AC Infinity controller you don't own, it's on/off only, and AC Infinity's whole advantage is variable‑speed control of their fans — which you have zero of. Every device you own is simple on/off, so you'd pay ~$197 to duplicate what your two Inkbirds already do — and your space heater is banned from the Control Plug anyway.

How the "AI controls it" part works — today
Safe · limited

Official HA MCP server

Built into Home Assistant. Claude can only touch the devices you explicitly expose — it can't reach your config, automations, or dashboards. The sane default.

Powerful · riskier

Community ha‑mcp

~90 tools — Claude can call any service, write automations, build dashboards. More reach, more ways to break something. Read‑only mode + per‑action approval + auto‑backups.

The cautionary tale

LLMs hallucinate

In a real test, Claude driving Home Assistant added wrong facts and, while "fixing" them, broke working sensors. Near a live grow: suggest‑and‑confirm, never autopilot.

Don't skip: real safety

  • 80% rule. Gear running for hours draws ≤80% of the circuit — ~1440W max on a 15A/120V line. Light + heater + dehumidifier add up fast.
  • Rate the switch to the load. A Shelly 1PM handles ~16A (~1750W @ 120V). Match the plug's amperage to the breaker. Never switch a heater with a bare hobby relay.
  • Keep the Inkbird failsafe. Hardware thermostats are the real safety net under any smart layer.

The smart first step — cheap, low‑risk, high‑value

Add one real sensor feeding your cockpit — a Pulse/AC Infinity, or an ESP32 + SCD41. No device switching yet. Prove the data pipeline into your app so the dials fill themselves and Claude can read real history. Then add smart‑plug control behind the hardware failsafes. That's the honest on‑ramp — value on day one, risk deferred until you're ready.
