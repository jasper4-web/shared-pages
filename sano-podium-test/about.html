Common questions

The things owners actually ask us

What if SANO doesn’t make it?
Fair thing to ask a new company. Your phone number is forwarded, never ported away from you — you keep ownership of it the whole time, and switching it back is a phone call to your carrier. Your customer list, call history and messages are exported to you on request at any time, not just if you cancel. Nothing we run creates a dependency you can’t unwind in a day.
What happens if it goes down?
Calls fall back to ringing your normal line, so the worst case is the situation you’re in today — not a dead number. We monitor it and you get told if something breaks, rather than finding out from a customer.
Do I have to learn a new system?
No. That is the point of the whole thing. You approve a written blueprint once, and after that the work shows up as bookings on your calendar and a monthly summary in plain English. There is no dashboard you are required to log into to get value.
Who actually writes what it says?
We draft it, you edit it, and nothing goes live until you sign off on four things: the greeting wording, what counts as an emergency, the price boundary, and the review copy. You can read the real sample of that document — it is the HVAC blueprint, in full.
Is the texting compliant?
Yes, and we treat it as a hard requirement rather than a detail. Every customer opts in, every message identifies your business, and STOP works instantly and permanently. We follow the TCPA and the carrier registration rules (10DLC), and we register your business before a single message goes out. If a message would not pass that bar, we do not send it.
