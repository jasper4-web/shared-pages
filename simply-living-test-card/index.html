The Four-Jar Test

Simply Living · all-purpose cleaner · settle dry-vs-liquid for good

Make four small ~8 oz (1 cup) jars. Let them sit 24 hours, then spray each on a greasy stovetop and a mirror. Each jar answers one question — the gel and the clog are findings, not failures.

The four jars
JAR A · the likely winner

Liquid castile

Your most likely real product (~90%)
  1. 8 oz warm water
  2. Stir in ½ tsp washing soda until clear
  3. (hard water?) stir in ½ tsp sodium citrate
  4. Let cool, gently stir in 1 Tbsp liquid castile — don't whip to suds
  5. 4 drops essential oil
Expect: stays clear, sprays clean, cuts grease.
JAR B · the dream, cold

Dry + cold shake

The "add water & shake" dream — the real test
  1. 8 oz cold tap water
  2. ½ tsp washing soda
  3. 1 tsp finely grated Dr. Bronner's bar
  4. Shake hard 1 minute
Expect it to gel ("soap snot"). That's the lesson — learned for pennies.
JAR C · dry, hot then cooled

Dry + hot water

Does even warm-water dry hold up?
  1. 8 oz hot water
  2. ½ tsp washing soda
  3. 1 tsp grated bar — stir till dissolved
  4. Let it cool and watch: does it re-gel?
Tells you if an "add warm water" label could ever work.
JAR D · the simple fallback

Soap-free degreaser

Could the truly-dry product skip soap entirely?
  1. 8 oz warm water
  2. ¾ tsp washing soda — only
  3. Stir till clear
Dissolves easily & cold. Does it cut grease well enough alone?

⚠ Safety — read once before you start

  • Never add vinegar, lemon, or raw citric acid to a soap jar — acid curdles soap into white clumps. (That's why we use sodium citrate, the neutralized form.)
  • Washing soda is alkaline (pH ~11). Wear gloves, keep it out of your eyes, don't breathe the dry powder.
  • Don't test on: aluminum, marble / soft stone, waxed or unsealed wood, delicate paint. Safe on: sealed counters, glazed tile, glass, stainless, sealed wood (lightly).
  • Glass needs its own step — soap streaks glass. Wipe with plain vinegar-water after, never in the jar.
